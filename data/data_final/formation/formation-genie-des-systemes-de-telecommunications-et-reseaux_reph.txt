This document concerns the training program in Telecommunications and Network Systems Engineering. The engineering cycle is titled Génie des Systèmes de Télécommunications et Réseaux, and the coordinator of the GSTR faculty is Prof. Samira Khoulji, whose e‑mail address is skhoulji@uae.ac.ma. The faculty focuses on a specialized branch within telecommunications and computer networking engineering, aiming to produce engineers capable of designing, implementing, maintaining, and improving telecommunication systems such as mobile telephony, data transmission, wireless networks, and communication protocols, as well as securing networks through authentication, access control, data encryption, intrusion detection, VPNs, and essential infrastructure like routers and switches. This program prepares students to meet the constantly changing demands of the telecommunications and networking sector by providing them with the technical expertise and understanding necessary to contribute meaningfully to this dynamic field.

Semester 1 includes courses in Computer Networks 1, Relational Databases, Probability, Statistics and Stochastic Calculus, Operations Research and Graph Theory, Languages and TEC 1, and Management 1. Semester 2 covers Transportation Networks, Digital Electronics, Object‑Oriented Modelling and Programming, Analog Electronics and Modulation, Signal Processing, and High‑Frequency Engineering. Semester 3 features Digital Communications, Computer Networks 2, Cellular and Wireless Networks, Digital Signal Processing and Digital Modulation, Radio Propagation and Optical Networks, Antennas, and Languages & TEC 2. Semester 4 offers C#, Object‑Relational Databases, Image and Speech Processing, Network Programming and Distributed Systems, Industrial Computing, Embedded Systems & Real‑Time, and Management 2. Semester 5 provides two options. Option 1 focuses on Telecommunications and Network Systems, Space Engineering, Advanced Digital Communication Systems, Network Security 2, Advanced Mobile and Wireless Networks, Embedded Systems 2 and Java Mobile, Communication 3, and Management 3. Option 2 concentrates on Cybersecurity and Cyberdefense, covering Cybersecurity, Auditing & Standards, WSN, IoT and Applications, Cryptography & Data Security, Cloud Computing Virtualization, SDN and Security, Cyberdefense and Cyberattack, Communication 3, and Management 3.
