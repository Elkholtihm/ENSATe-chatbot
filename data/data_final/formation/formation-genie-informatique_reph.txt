This document presents the curriculum for the Computer Engineering cycle, coordinated by Professor Yacine El Younoussi, whose email is yacine.info@gmail.com. In the first semester students take courses in Languages and Communication I, Management I, Probabilities, Statistics and Stochastic Calculus, Computer Networks I, Information Systems and Relational Database Foundations, and Graph Theory and Operations Research. The second semester includes Digital Electronics, Web Programming I, Advanced C Programming and Complexity, Operating Systems, Language Theory and Compilation, and Computer Architecture and Assembly. The third semester covers Object-Oriented Modeling and Programming, Network Programming and Information Security, Computer Networks II, Software Engineering Methodologies, Languages and Communication II, and Management II. In the fourth semester students study Artificial Intelligence, Database Administration and Optimization, Web Programming II, Advanced Java, Computer Vision, and Relational-Object and Distributed Databases. The fifth semester offers three options: the SIAD track features Information Systems and Decision Applications, Integration Systems and Enterprise Software, Business Intelligence, Information Systems Urbanization, System Programming, DotNet and JEE Technologies, Languages and Communication III, and Management III; the Software Engineering track includes Software Testing and Reliability, Information Systems Urbanization and Workflow, Formal Modeling Languages and Verification, DotNet and JEE, Real-Time Embedded Operating Systems, Languages and Communication III, and Management III; the Network and Systems Engineering track comprises Network Security, Network Management and Administration, Quality of Service and Network Optimization, Wireless and Sensor Networks, DotNet and JEE, Languages and Communication III, and Management III. The program concludes with a Final Project.
