The document concerns the clubs of ENSA Tetouan. The ETP club, Presse ENSA Tétouan, brings together students to serve their school by creating a network through a website that shares exclusive information about students, clubs, and administrative announcements, fostering interaction between students and administrators while faithfully reporting events and updates. CETEC, a non‑profit club founded in 2012, is headquartered at ENSA and aims to nurture interpersonal skills and communication, public speaking, leadership, cultural awareness, and intellectual exchange among engineering students, especially those from sub‑Saharan regions, encouraging reading, writing, and diverse perspectives. Civil Engineering Club, created in 2016, offers engineering students a glimpse into the breadth of design careers and motivates them to pursue the field through learning and shared experience. ECHECS ENSATé, a sports club established by engineering students, focuses on promoting chess as a means to intellectual excellence, developing skills through teaching, practice, and competition, and is prepared to manage a structured program to achieve top performance and inspire others. Enactus ENSA Tétouan, part of Enactus Morocco since 2014, targets students with socially driven projects, building partnerships between local communities around Tétouan and higher education while upholding Enactus principles to prepare future ethical, entrepreneurial, and socially responsible leaders. ENSART, founded in 2016, is a student organization that gathers creative and inventive members to foster socio‑cultural growth, support artistic expression across the school, and collaborate with other clubs to enrich the extracurricular landscape. GRAFICA, one of the newest clubs created under the guidance of President Yasir Wahid and supervisor Mr. Belmir Brahim, unites students passionate about graphic design, aiming to sharpen creative skills, integrate engineering students into campus life, and offer collective video and photographic learning opportunities that meet the school’s graphic art needs. Hult Prize, a global competition, has taken place at ENSA Tetouan about two years ago and hosted two OnCampus Finals, the first held online due to the pandemic and preceded by workshops to help participants refine their pitches; the journey develops leadership, teamwork, and emotional intelligence as participants work through a full year of idea development, regional and national contests, and accelerators. InfoTech, a non‑profit scientific club founded in 2009 by engineering students, creates a supportive environment for academic growth through events and activities related to computer science and information technology. AFI, the “Future Engineers” association established in 2019 to formalize the Moovit club that began in 2016, is a social organization dedicated to helping the most vulnerable, driven by a group of motivated, sharing‑inclined individuals who aim to bring hope and support through various events. LEAVE IT HERE focuses on raising collective awareness of psychological and mental health among engineering students, maintaining anonymity and confidentiality while fostering solidarity, grace, and tolerance to create a supportive environment for personal and professional development. LOGISTICA, a non‑profit student organization, brings engineering students together around supply chain management, sharing knowledge and information to sharpen their skills and facilitate professional integration. MASJID, founded in 2013 by a group of students, is a non‑political extracurricular club that encourages spiritual growth and social responsibility, integrating religious values into daily student life, promoting self‑improvement, community support, and preparing members to become responsible citizens. Mécatronique, initiated in 2009 by students under Ms. Oussama Mahboub, is a scientific club that instills creativity and innovation through multidisciplinary projects involving mechanics, electronics, computing, and automation, linking theory with real‑world practice and preparing members for local, national, and international competitions. Rotaract, a service club for young adults aged 18 to 30, was created in 1968 in the United States and has since expanded worldwide; the ENSA Tétouan branch, formed in 2016, gathers proactive engineering students who embrace charitable and ethical values, encouraging community engagement, personal growth, and friendship while addressing material, social, and cultural needs. SPORT, established in 2010 by students from various cohorts, serves as a meeting place for all students regardless of discipline, background, or physical ability, promoting balanced engineering graduates who are socially adept and adaptable to diverse work situations. Wirenet, a non‑profit club founded for and by ENSA Tétouan engineering students under Professor Otman Aghzout El Idrissi, promotes the Telecommunications and Networking Engineering discipline, bringing together the necessary resources to help students launch their professional careers. AI Geeks, a community of students at ENSA Tétouan specializing in Big Data and Artificial Intelligence, explores, learns, and shares knowledge in these rapidly evolving fields.
